About The Stroke Risk Predictor

Understanding our risk assessment tool and how it works

Understanding Stroke Risk

A stroke occurs when blood flow to part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, preventing brain tissue from getting oxygen and nutrients. Brain cells begin to die within minutes, making stroke a medical emergency that requires immediate attention.

According to the World Health Organization, stroke is the second leading cause of death globally and a major cause of disability. Early identification of risk factors and preventive measures can significantly reduce the incidence of stroke.

This application leverages machine learning technology to assess an individual's risk of stroke based on various health and demographic factors. The prediction is based on a K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) model trained on historical patient data, allowing for personalized risk assessment.

Key Risk Factors for Stroke

Age

Stroke risk doubles each decade after age 55. While strokes can occur at any age, the risk increases significantly with advancing years due to natural changes in blood vessels and heart function.

Hypertension

High blood pressure damages blood vessels over time, making them more likely to rupture or become blocked. It's the single most important modifiable risk factor for stroke.

Glucose Levels

Diabetes and elevated blood sugar significantly increase stroke risk by damaging blood vessels and promoting atherosclerosis. Effective blood sugar management reduces this risk substantially.

Heart Disease

Heart conditions like atrial fibrillation can lead to blood clots that may travel to the brain and cause an ischemic stroke. Proper heart health management is essential for stroke prevention.

About Our Model

The KNN Algorithm

Our application employs the K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) algorithm, selected after evaluating multiple metrics including ROC-AUC, F1 score, and Recall. KNN predicts stroke risk by analyzing how similar a patient's health profile is to known cases in our training dataset.

This model was chosen for its strong performance in healthcare predictions, interpretability, and ability to handle mixed data types effectively. The algorithm works by identifying the most similar historical cases and using them to make predictions about new patients.

Data and Features

The prediction model analyzes ten critical factors proven to be associated with stroke risk:

  • Age
  • Average glucose level
  • BMI (Body Mass Index)
  • Hypertension status
  • Heart disease history
  • Gender
  • Marital status
  • Work type
  • Residence type
  • Smoking status
